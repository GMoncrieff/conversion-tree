Estimating Demand for Land Cover Change

The demand component of conversion threat forecasting quantifies how much change is expected to occur over a given time period. This represents the total area or rate of conversion that will need to be allocated spatially across your study area. Demand estimation is a critical first step, as it constrains the total amount of threat in your forecast.

Demand can be estimated through various approaches depending on your data availability:

  • Historical trends: Extrapolating past rates of change into the future
  • Scenario-based projections: Using socioeconomic scenarios (e.g., IPCC RCP scenarios) to estimate future land use requirements
  • Expert estimates: Leveraging existing studies or expert knowledge about expected change
  • Empirical analysis: Deriving rates from multi-temporal land cover maps

Accurate demand estimation is crucial because it constrains the total amount of change allocated across your study area. Overestimating demand can lead to unrealistic projections, while underestimating may miss important conversion hotspots.
