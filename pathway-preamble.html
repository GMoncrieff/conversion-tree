Selecting Your Threat Forecasting Pathway

Threats to conservation features are not spatially or temporally uniform, and effective planning depends on forecasts that identify where and when future pressures are most likely to occur. The first step in developing a conversion threat forecast is determining which modeling pathway is most appropriate for your context.

Conversion refers to the complete transformation of one land cover type to another (e.g., forest to agriculture, natural habitat to urban). This represents a categorical change in land use and is the primary threat in many conservation contexts.

Degradation refers to the decline in quality or condition of a land cover type without complete conversion (e.g., forest thinning, habitat fragmentation, loss of ecosystem function). Degradation is often more subtle but can significantly impact conservation values.

Your modeling pathway depends on:

  • Whether you're forecasting conversion or degradation
  • The spatial scale of your analysis (global/continental vs. national/regional)
  • Whether you want to extrapolate historical patterns or explore alternative scenarios
  • Your data availability and capacity to develop custom models
